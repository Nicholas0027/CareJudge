\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We studied \emph{protocol stability} as a per-instance reliability signal for LLM-as-a-judge and characterized its governing property: the signal is \emph{competence-gated}. A verdict that changes under semantically equivalent rubrics, orderings, or resampling is unreliable---but only when the judge is capable enough that its instabilities track genuine difficulty; below a competence threshold the signal inverts and a near-random judge is stably wrong. CARE-Judge operationalizes this by fusing the three instability families into a learned calibrator with inherited selective-risk control~\citep{jung2025trust,badshah2026scope}. The same mechanism accounts for fusion helping in the mid-capability band (DeepSeek-V4 and Qwen2.5-7B), tying a saturated frontier judge, and inverting below the threshold; the pre-specified mid-capability test is significant while the pooled average is diluted by regimes where fusion cannot help.

\paragraph{Practical recommendation.}
A simple rule follows: deploy protocol-stability abstention for mid-capability judges (roughly $0.65$--$0.8$ raw agreement), where fusion buys the largest risk-controlled coverage; for a frontier judge with near-perfect verbalized confidence the extra perturbation calls are optional; and below the competence threshold, abstain by default rather than trust stable verdicts.

\paragraph{Limitations and Future Work.}
Our risk control is empirical (a data-dependent threshold validated by measured violation rates, not a family-wise-corrected guarantee) and requires reference labels; stability does not imply correctness, and a judge with a shared bias can defeat every signal at once. The ladder isolates size but not recipe; a crossed size$\times$recipe study, a conditionally-calibrated cascade with a formal guarantee, and extension to pointwise scoring and human-annotation triage are future work.

\paragraph{Ethical considerations.}
Abstention is not neutral: a system that abstains disproportionately on certain topics or slices could silently skew downstream evaluation. Competence-gating makes this explicit---CARE-Judge abstains rather than emitting confident wrong verdicts where a weak judge is unreliable---but deployers should audit \emph{which} items are abstained on, not just aggregate coverage, and should not treat an accepted verdict as ground truth in high-stakes settings.
