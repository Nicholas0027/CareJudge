\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We studied \emph{protocol stability} as a per-instance reliability signal for LLM-as-a-judge and identified its governing property: the signal is \emph{competence-gated}. A verdict that changes under semantically equivalent rubrics, orderings, or resampling is unreliable---but only when the judge is capable enough that its instabilities track genuine difficulty; below a competence floor the signal inverts and a near-random judge is stably wrong. CARE-Judge operationalizes this finding, fusing the three instability families into a learned calibrator paired with inherited selective-risk control~\citep{jung2025trust,badshah2026scope}. Read as one picture across the capability spectrum, the same mechanism explains why fusion helps most on mid-capability judges (significant on DeepSeek-V4, replicated on Qwen2.5-7B), ties a saturated frontier judge, and inverts below the floor---so a non-significant global average is a prediction of the theory, not a counterexample to it.

\paragraph{Practical recommendation.}
For practitioners, the results suggest a simple rule: deploy protocol-stability abstention for mid-capability judges (roughly $0.65$--$0.8$ raw agreement), where fusion buys the largest risk-controlled coverage; for a frontier judge whose verbalized confidence is already near-perfect, base confidence suffices and the extra perturbation calls are optional; and for a near-random judge below the competence floor, abstain by default rather than trust stable verdicts.

\paragraph{Limitations and Future Work.}
Our risk control is empirical (threshold chosen data-dependently, validated by measured violation rates, not a family-wise-corrected guarantee) and requires reference labels. Stability does not imply correctness, and a judge with a shared bias can defeat every signal simultaneously. The ladder isolates size but not training recipe; a fully crossed size$\times$recipe study is future work, as are a conditionally-calibrated cascade with a formal guarantee and extension to pointwise scoring and human-annotation triage.

\paragraph{Ethical considerations.}
Selective judging is a safety mechanism, but abstention is not neutral: a system that abstains disproportionately on certain topics or demographic slices could silently skew downstream evaluation. Competence-gating makes this explicit rather than hidden---CARE-Judge abstains, rather than emitting confident wrong verdicts, exactly where a weak judge is unreliable---but deployers should audit \emph{which} items are abstained on, not only the aggregate coverage, and should not treat an accepted verdict as ground truth in high-stakes settings.
