\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We studied \emph{protocol stability} as a per-instance reliability signal for LLM-as-a-judge and found it to be \emph{competence-gated}. A verdict that changes under semantically equivalent rubrics, orderings, or resampling is unreliable---but only when the judge is accurate enough on the task that its instabilities track difficulty; below an accuracy threshold the signal inverts and a near-random judge is stably wrong. CARE-Judge implements this by fusing the three instability families into a learned calibrator with inherited selective-risk control~\citep{jung2025trust,badshah2026scope}, helping most in the mid-accuracy band, tying a saturated frontier judge, and inverting below the threshold.

\paragraph{Practical recommendation.}
Deploy protocol-stability abstention for mid-accuracy judges ($\approx0.65$--$0.8$ raw agreement), where fusion buys the largest risk-controlled coverage; the extra perturbation calls are optional for a frontier judge, and below the threshold one should abstain by default.

\paragraph{Limitations and Future Work.}
Our risk control is empirical: the Clopper--Pearson bound is exact but the threshold is chosen data-dependently, so we validate it by measured violation rates rather than a family-wise theorem; coupling the acceptance rule to a selective-conformal procedure with a conditional guarantee~\citep{xu2025selective,bao2024cap} would close this gap. CARE also needs task-specific labels to fit and calibrate, and these do not transfer freely (Appendix~\ref{app:transfer}), so it is a per-task reliability layer, not a task-agnostic gate. Base confidence is verbalized (log-probabilities are not universally exposed), which may conflate verbosity or overconfidence with uncertainty across judge families---though our stability signals are designed not to depend on it. The negative control uses two judges on one dataset; broader replication would harden it. Finally, stability does not imply correctness---a shared-bias judge can defeat every signal at once. A crossed size$\times$recipe study, a formally-guaranteed cascade, and pointwise scoring are future work; and since abstention is not neutral, deployers should audit \emph{which} items it excludes.
